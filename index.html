The Spurs billed Bexar County more than $250,000 to use AT&T Center as voting site, records show

The San Antonio Spurs billed local elections officials more than $250,000 for using the county-owned AT&T Center as a voting site during the November election, documents show.In an unprecedented move pushed by county officials, the AT&T Center opened as a “mega voting center” as a way for San Antonio voters to safely cast their ballots amid the coronavirus pandemic and to expand voting access.

The $250,000 price tag far exceeded the costs for any of Bexar County’s other polling places. The county hasn’t yet paid the Spurs — and county leaders said they would try to figure out whether the price is justified.
